Design Strategies for Augmented Reality based Learning Experience in Classrooms

Ongoing Doctoral Thesis, IIT Bombay, July 2017 - Present

The use of ICT mediums is prevalent in Indian schools nowadays. However, they still involve instructor-mediated instructions using technological tools. After showing related examples or demonstrations to students using technology mediums, the students are given textbook problems to solve, making it difficult for students to think and solve the problems beyond the ones given in the textbook. There are various abstract concepts that need visualisation. In the process of teaching such concepts, it cannot be assured whether the students have learned those concepts. Moreover, the evaluation of students’ learning is done using formative tests after a chapter gets over. The current system lacks in providing the students with immediate feedback to validate whether the student has been able to learn the taught concept. Thus, the thesis focuses in designing a student-centred classroom solution ScholAR using one of the emerging technologies called Augmented Reality (AR) to provide interactive and immersive learning experience to students. The research work is focused at answering questions such as what must be the design considerations in designing such an experience? What components must be considered for providing a good user experience while using an interactive visualisation aiding tool in the classroom? How to assure instant evaluation of the learned concepts using the designed interactive visualisation tool? What framework must be followed by the designers to design such a classroom-based interactive teacher- student learning tool? Based on the generated framework, we are designing an AR application to validate the framework and understand the students learning impact, usability, interactivity and perceived usefulness.
